RansomHub Ransomware Threat Analysis — Risk & IOC Assessment

Module 12 Learning Activity: Malware in the News  |  St. John's University  |  April 2026

Threat analysis of RansomHub, a Ransomware-as-a-Service operation that compromised over 210 organizations across healthcare, government, water utilities, and financial services. Grounded in the FBI, CISA, and HHS joint advisory (AA24-242A), the analysis examined the group's attack lifecycle, indicators of compromise, and organizational impact.

  • Documented network-based IOCs including C2 over port 443, lateral movement via RDP/SMB, Tor-based communications, and data exfiltration via MEGAsync and WinSCP.
  • Analyzed three actively exploited CVEs: CVE-2023-3519 (Citrix NetScaler RCE), CVE-2023-22515 (Atlassian Confluence), and CVE-2020-1472 ZeroLogon.
  • Identified persistence mechanisms including scheduled task masquerading, HKCU registry run key modifications, and abuse of RMM tools (AnyDesk, Atera).
  • Developed SIEM and EDR detection recommendations tied to VSS deletion commands, PowerShell/WMI abuse, and the double-extortion kill chain.
CISA Advisory AA24-242A · SIEM/EDR Concepts · CVE Analysis · Threat Intelligence
